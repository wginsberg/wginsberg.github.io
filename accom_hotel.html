La Casa del Retoño

Previous Previous

What can you expect from 'La Casa del Retoño'?

In Casa del Retoño you can enjoy the comfort, tranquility and warmth of a Mexican family house built with typical Mexican materials which guarantee an unforgettable stay. You will see historic and architectonic details, originals of México and artisan decoration that will take you back in time and learn more of the history of handcraft and pottery, which gives Tlaquepaque its name.

What's 'Casa del Retoño' like?

La Casa del Retoño has 8 rooms, each with full bathrooms, radio alarm clock, fan, telephone, wireless internet and with a capacity for 2 or 3 guests. We also have one suite with capacity for up to 4 guests, it has a full bathroom, kitchenette, radio alarm clock, TV, telephone and fan.

The breakfast area is located in the main corridor of the house, under blossom trees that will let you enjoy your breakfast, a cup of coffee or your favorite tea in the cool shade and tranquility of La Casa del Retoño.

You will also find a charming terrace and garden with big flowering trees, both exclusive for our guests where you can rest while listening to the song birds and have the benefit of the peace and quiet that can only be offered by a typical Mexican house. It is the ideal place for reading your favorite book, relax and get in touch with yourself.

Quinta Don Jose

"Quinta Don Jose Boutique Hotel" offers you a beautiful and relaxing setting to base your visit to the Guadalajara area!"

Previous Previous

Travel writers and travelers both agree that basing their Guadalajara visit in Tlaquepaque's "small town atmosphere" makes for a more enjoyable experience! So why not consider "QDJ" instead of a large Guadalajara hotel?

Quinta Don Jose puts you only 15 minutes from Guadalajara's Historic Downtown and only steps from Tlaquepaque's Old Town District. Tlaquepaque is a charming Mexican arts and crafts village where you'll discover a truly superb shopping experience. Stroll cobble streets lined with a variety of delightful galleries, unique museums, and inviting restaurants housed in 18th century colonial mansions. Combining your visit to Guadalajara's historic and cultural attractions with Tlaquepaque's arts, crafts, music and food will bring you the full experience of what is known as "The Most Mexican City"!

After a day of sightseeing and shopping, return to "QDJ" to relax by the pool with a Margarita and re-energize for an evening of culinary discovery in Tlaquepaque.

"TlaquePasta", our own cozy Mexican/Italian restaurant is ready to greet you! Allow our Chef Patrick, to prepare for you traditional Mexican dishes or enjoy the only Italian menu in our area. Delicious food prepared only with the highest quality ingredients and "service a la QDJ"!
